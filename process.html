Visual and verbal learning

To learn new things it is nice to know how you learn best and which approach to take in order to learn new things. For me, learning is all about the visual and the verbal aspect. Therefore I like to sketch by hand when I do mockups, read about how to code, and not just being told how to do it, write lists to myself on how I will approach a project and so on.


Making errors

Last but not least I need to fail and make errors in order to remember what to do and what not to do. I must admit I will never be a fan of the feeling of frustration it evokes, but it is certainly an effective step in the learning process.

Theory

Design and communication are often two sides of the same coin. A good design can get messy if the communication is not clear and structured communication can become complex if the design is not there to guide the user.

One of the theories I enjoy applying in projects is the Design Thinking theory because it helps me create an overview of the project process. It is so simple and yet so complex. The structure of the theory is simple and only contains five steps; empathize, define, ideate, prototype and test. It is a never ending process where you can go through the five steps as many times you need (or for as long you can).
